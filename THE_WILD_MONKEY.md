# The Wild Monkey

**Author:** Donatus Prosper Ihechi  
**Published:** September 2025

---

In the heart of the deep green jungle of Kairu, where trees touched the clouds and rivers sang through the rocks, all animals lived under the rule of the mighty lion, King Zubari.

Zubari was strong. His roar could silence storms, and every creature feared and respected him. For many seasons, no one questioned his rule… except one.

Deep in the jungle, there lived a small monkey named Moki. Unlike the others, Moki was not strong or frightening. He was clever, fast, and full of ideas that no one understood.

While the lion ruled with strength, Moki watched, listened, and learned.

## The Problem in the Jungle

One dry season, food became scarce. The lion ordered all animals to bring him their best harvest. Many animals suffered, but none dared to complain.

Moki saw this and said: *"Strength without fairness will break the jungle someday."*

The animals laughed and said, "What can a small monkey do against a lion?"

## The Monkey's Plan

Instead of fighting, Moki used wisdom. He helped animals find food, solve conflicts, and avoid danger in secret.

## The Turning Point

When a great storm struck the jungle, confusion spread. The lion's orders failed, but Moki guided everyone to safety.

## The Rise of the Wild Monkey

After the storm, the animals chose wisdom over strength. They crowned Moki as their leader.

## Lesson

**True leadership is not about strength, but wisdom, fairness, and service.**

---

*A timeless fable exploring the power of wisdom and integrity in leadership.*
